Strategic read

What the data points to

ThemeWhat the data shows
BrandThe decline is auction position. From mid-May, CPC rose from ~$10 to $38–65 and impressions lost to rank reached 45–62%, with top-of-page under 25%. Recovering top-of-page calls for impression-share budget on the brand term.
MQL concentration78% of May paid-search MQLs (117 of 150) came from GS_NB_AI and Content-Development, both scheduled to pause June 15 and June 22. The seven product campaigns produced 18 MQLs in May. If the pauses hold before the product campaigns scale, MQL volume falls further.
SyndicateHighest-spend product campaign ($12.6K in May) at 1 MQL. Volume is building but not yet replacing the broad-campaign MQLs.
Competitor campaignsAbout $8K over the period with 0 MQLs across the three. Clearest line for redeployment or rework.
June decisionThe pause timing is the key call: hold the dates and accept a lower June MQL number while product campaigns build, or delay the pauses to lift the product baseline first. Best made with James.

● Summary

Impressions up, clicks down

Through May 6 the account tracked with April. From May 7, clicks and conversions stepped down while spend continued. From May 14, Brand impressions rose while Brand clicks fell and Brand CPC increased — an impressions-up / clicks-down (auction-position) pattern. The final week of May was a deliberate budget reduction, which accounts for part of the month-end low.

Exact account configuration changes and their dates are available in the Google Ads Change History: account 6333716782 → Changes → May 1 to date.
