What changed · why · what to expect

Fewer leads on purpose. Better-fit pipeline.

Over the last month we deliberately cut the paid searches that were sending leads which disqualified about 80% of the time. Lead volume is down by design while we clean things up and bring new channels online. This page walks through what changed, why, and what to expect — with answers for the questions reps and prospects will ask.

The short version

What was wrong → what we did → what's next

● What was wrong

Half the leads were junk

About half of paid leads came from broad "AI" searches that disqualified roughly 80% of the time. A generic "syndicate" search and a brand tracking break were quietly draining the brand campaign, and a few over-broad negative keywords were blocking good searches too.

● What we did

Cleaned up and re-aimed

Cut the junk searches, started feeding Sales Accepted Lead data back into Google so we can optimize toward the leads sales accepts, rescoped negatives to protect good keywords, added Microsoft and a stronger competitor page, and began ramping tightly-targeted product campaigns (Flare, Create, Syndicate).

● What's next

Rebuild volume, keep the fit

Demand Gen + YouTube is now live (launched Jul 1) rebuilding awareness and a retargeting pool, product campaigns are scaling, and we're fixing the brand tracking and the "syndicate" leak and moving LinkedIn to thought-leadership video. The broad AI campaign winds down only after these backfill it — no cliff.

The offense

How we're bringing volume back

Each lever adds qualified volume from a different angle, so we're not relying on any single campaign.

Demand Gen + YouTube Live · Jul 1

Now live. The awareness campaign launched around July 1 and drove ~2.3M impressions in its first week, building top-of-funnel awareness and a retargeting pool to feed the funnel. Early days — it is not converting yet, which is expected while it learns. (A Flare-specific video is still needed for product-level targeting.)

Microsoft Ads — Brand + Flare Live Tracking fix

Incremental reach at a lower cost than Google. A tracking break since around May 27 is being repaired before we read results.

Product campaigns: Flare · Create · Syndicate Ramping

Tightly targeted phrase/exact keywords, replacing the broad junk with better-fit leads. Volume is building.

Sales-Accepted-Lead optimization Evaluating

SAL data started flowing into Google on May 7. Before we shift campaigns to optimize on SALs, we're gathering SAL volume by campaign — today most SALs come from the two broad campaigns, so a blanket shift would just chase those. We move each campaign over once it has the SAL volume to support it.

Brand recovery In progress

Repair the brand conversion tracking and block the generic "syndicate" search that was draining budget at zero conversions — so brand spend works again.

Negative-keyword rescope Done · Jun 9

Over-broad negatives were blocking good searches like "documentation software." Rescoped to phrase/exact on June 9 so they cut the junk without blocking the good.

LinkedIn Shifting

Moving from single-image demo ads to engagement and thought-leadership video.

Competitor — Flare vs Paligo Live · May 20

A stronger comparison landing page to convert competitor-intent traffic.

Bear with us

The 30 / 60 / 90 recovery

A dip while the account relearns, then volume climbs back as the new channels ramp. The pattern below is what we expect, not an exact forecast.

Now · 0–30 days

Relearn & warm up

The account re-optimizes after the cleanup. Volume dips while the new channels warm. We watch acceptance rate and disqualifications, not raw lead count.

30–60 days

Traction

Product campaigns, Microsoft, and Demand Gen gain traction. Qualified volume starts climbing back, with better fit than before.

60–90 days

Backfill & wind-down

New channels reach enough scale to backfill the broad-AI volume. Only then do we wind that campaign down — no cliff, better overall mix.

Brand search

Why brand got expensive

Through April, brand search held top position at a normal cost per click. From the week of May 11 competitors bid it up and a generic "syndicate" search drained the budget — cost per click jumped 3–6×, and we lost most impressions to rank, not to budget. Repairing the tracking and blocking that generic search are part of brand recovery.

For the conversation

Questions you'll get — and answers

Q"Where did our leads go?"
We cut junk searches that disqualified about 80% of the time. Those leads weren't reaching sales as real opportunities anyway. The actual pipeline is intact.
Q"Fewer leads — isn't that bad?"
Quality is up. Qualified leads more than doubled on Flare (~24 → ~56), and about 70% of May's MQLs were accepted by sales. Fewer leads, far better fit.
Q"Won't turning off the AI campaign hurt us?"
It's about half our leads, but mostly junk. We're bringing new channels up first — Microsoft and the product campaigns, plus Demand Gen + YouTube now live as of July 1 — so qualified volume holds. We only wind the AI campaign down once those backfill it.
Q"When does volume come back?"
Over 30 / 60 / 90 days as the new channels ramp. We review weekly on Sales Accepted Leads and disqualifications, and adjust if quality slips.
● The bottom line

Down on purpose, then back up — with a better mix

We deliberately traded raw lead volume for fit: cut the junk searches, started measuring the leads sales actually accept, and fixed what was quietly draining brand. Volume is down ~28% by design while qualified leads more than doubled on our top product and ~70% of leads were accepted by sales.

The recovery is sequenced so there's no cliff — Microsoft and the product campaigns ramp first, Demand Gen + YouTube went live July 1, and the broad AI campaign only eases down once they backfill the volume. June turned the corner (the week of June 8 was the strongest in weeks) and into July search is holding steady with better fit while the new awareness channel builds.
