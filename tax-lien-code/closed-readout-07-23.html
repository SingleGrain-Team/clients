Insights & Actions

  • Efficiency is strong; the gap is volume. Las Vegas brought cost per registration down 20% year over year and Fort Lauderdale delivered on 22% less spend, both under budget, so cost is not the constraint. Action: on the live events we are concentrating budget into the final window to turn that efficiency into more registrations before close.
  • Interest-based targeting is the most efficient lead source and was underweighted. It produced the lowest cost per registration but held the smallest share of budget. Action: rebalancing budget toward interest-based audiences on the next launches.
  • Attendance is driven by registration timing, not ad frequency. On Wednesday's question: our retargeting ran at much higher ad frequency than prospecting but did not produce a higher show rate, so heavier exposure is not what lifts attendance. What moves it is how close to the event someone registers, with last-window sign-ups attending at a materially higher rate. Action: strengthening the reminder sequence for early registrants and back-loading spend so more registrations land in the high-intent final days.
  • Proven creative carries attendance. Blue Event Dates static and Brian's direct call-out videos drove the most attended registrants in both markets. Action: leading rotation with these proven concepts and building fresh variations of them to stay ahead of fatigue.
