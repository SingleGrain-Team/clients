What we are doing about it
Three in-flight initiatives, each addressing a different point in the registration-to-attendance chain. Brian creative refresh qualifies intent upstream of registration so the people who sign up are pre-screened for outcome interest, not just curiosity. Isolated concept ad sets give a clean per-concept read on which creative angle holds attention through the pre-event window. Evergreen post-registration nurture keeps registrants engaged from sign-up day through event day. The non-linear budget test on Greenville stacks on top — compressing the registration-to-event gap to reduce the decay window in the first place.
Greenville non-linear budget test — baseline + tracking
Greenville launches Sunday 5/24 with the back-loaded budget curve: 15% spend in days 1-7, 25% in days 8-14, 60% in the final stretch. Hypothesis: shorter registration-to-event gap lifts show rate by reducing the decay window between sign-up and event date. We track raw regs and final show rate against Greenville's own pre-test baseline and the broader 2026 series-format performance.

Why Greenville is the right test market

Greenville has almost no series-format history on this account — two prior workshops only, both small samples, both with decent show rates. That makes it the right place to run an aggressive budget-shape test. If the back-loaded curve underperforms, we're not breaking a market that's already working — we're learning on a market with effectively zero baseline to disrupt. The two prior events do tell us the audience there isn't fundamentally hostile to the offer, which means a poor result on the test is more likely to be about budget shape than about market fit.
